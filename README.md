Pipe rack — 17 SVG pipes drawn at graduated heights (tall bass, short treble), with bronze flange rings on the larger pipes, a mouth slot near the base, and a cap line at the top. When a note sounds, the pipe body glows blue, the cap brightens, and a smoke/air puff animates off the top.
Keyboard — same as the theremin's. ASDFGHJK for white keys, WETYU for black. Z/X shifts octave. The octave wheel scrolls on the left.
Water chamber — a live animated water surface that agitates based on how many notes are held. More voices = choppier water, more bubbles, higher level. There's a pressure gauge on the right.
The sound itself — four registers, each modeling a different pipe type:

Principal — sawtooth through bandpass, slightly nasal metal flue pipe
Tibia — triangle wave + looping breath noise at the pipe mouth, hollow and airy
Bombarde — sawtooth through a waveshaper for reed buzz, penetrating and reedy
Mixture — four simultaneous ranks (unison, octave, 2nd octave, quint) with slight detuning

Stops:

Tremulant — amplitude wobble at ~5.5Hz, the organ tremulant effect
Hydraulus — second very slow LFO (~0.3Hz) for the water pressure swell, the defining hydraulis character
Drone — adds a sub-bass pipe an octave below your lowest held note
